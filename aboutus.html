About Us

We are a team of Humanistic & Integrative Psychotherapists and Counsellors, all accredited by the Irish Association of Humanistic & Integrative Pyschotherapy.

All our therapists trained with the highly regarded Flatstone Institute in Cork. Flatstone provided an iexperientially-based training, with a focus on creating the optimum environment for in-depth relational learning. Flatstone's aim was to develop psychotherapists that could offer true healing and life-enhancing psychotherapy.

As Humanistic & Integrative Psychotherapists, we work in a relational way with each person, drawing from a variety of therapeutic modalities. We aim to guide and support you towards self-discovery and healing, in both practical and nurturing ways. As a team we are dedicated to creating safe, confidential and non-judgemental spaces. We promote openness, curiosity, authenticity, self-acceptance, self-care and nurturing.

Each member of our team:
  • Runs their own private practice at various locations in Cork and Kerry, where we offer in-person sessions.
  • Provides online therapy and counselling.
  • Offers personalised one-to-one support, tailored to the individual’s specific needs.
  • Offers therapeutic group options, providing a variety of supportive and collaborative environments for personal growth.
  • Works with adults over the age of 18.
  • Is fully insured.

About Ann

I’m delighted you’re on this website, because even though you are probably struggling with different parts of your life right now, you’re here because you’re looking for something to change, and you’re looking for a therapist to help you with these changes.

I am a fully accredited Counsellor and Psychotherapist with the Irish Association of Humanistic and Integrative Psychotherapists (IAHIP) and the Addiction Counsellors of Ireland (ACI).

I am an experienced facilitator of Smart Recovery addiction recovery groups and of AFINET’s 5 Step Family Support Programme, for those affected by addiction.

I am a sessional therapist with AHR Employee Assistance Programme, and I am an affiliated counsellor with Dunlewey Addiction Service.

I work with people one-to-one or in groups, both face-to-face and online.

My Approach

Carolyn Spring (2019) puts it that “people come to therapy, when they’ve experienced a wound, a relational trauma, that has been been perpetrated or caused by another human being. And so people need to have a reparative experience, an opposite experience, where instead of hurt and harm and abuse, people experience care and compassion and empathy. That then has the potential to change the way they view other people, themselves, and the nature of the world”.

The aim of my Psychotherapy and Counselling work is to allow you to deepen awareness of yourself without judgement and with compassionate curiosity.

I centre this therapeutic work around the relational experiences between us in the therapy session, whilst also using your experience of yourself, your relationships and the environment around you. I encourage you to develop an open curiosity for how you are in therapy and in the world around you.

About Mary

My name is Mary, and I am a fully accredited psychotherapist, counsellor, and pet grief coach. I specialize as a Humanistic Integrated Psychotherapist, working collaboratively with clients to help them realize their true potential, abilities, and unique qualities.

I bring a wealth of experience to my practice. I volunteered with St. Vincent de Paul (S.V.P.) for seven years and with Foróige for nine years. I also trained as a facilitator with the Social Health Education Project, where I led groups in personal development, stress management, and communication. Additionally, I have facilitated aftercare groups for families coping with addiction, recovery, and relapse, and I provided counseling to individuals in addiction treatment centers.

My Approach

My aim is to provide clients with a safe, genuine, confidential, and accepting space that is free from judgment. I work at the client’s pace, ensuring they feel heard, seen, and supported in addressing the issues causing discomfort or distress in their daily lives.

I have extensive experience helping clients navigate life’s challenges, including addiction, supporting families of individuals struggling with addiction, stress, anxiety, relationship difficulties (whether family or work-related), and everyday obstacles. My approach is empathetic and tailored to each individual, providing practical tools and emotional support to help them overcome these difficulties and move toward a more fulfilling life.

I also have a deep love and respect for animals and understand the heartbreak of losing a beloved pet. For this reason, I offer a safe space to support clients through the grief process.

I work with adults aged 18 and over, offering both face-to-face and online sessions.

About Mary

I am a fully accredited Humanistic and Integrative Psychotherapist, with experience of providing individual and group counselling and psychotherapy over many years. The psychotherapeutic approach and modality I use is tailored to the individual’s specific needs and what works best for them.

Being a mother of four adolescents and young adults, I have a particular interest in working with mothers and the unique needs and demands of motherhood. I offer face to face and online one-to-one support for those experiencing challenging times in motherhood, as well as facilitating mothers support groups.

My Approach

My aim is to gain a real understanding of what the person is experiencing, and to establish a relationship between us, where the person feels safe, heard, understood and supported in finding a useful and empowering path forward.

I offer compassionate support to help uncover hidden and unconscious beliefs, thoughts and feelings that may be negatively impacting on the person’s quality and enjoyment of life.

I support adults going through many challenging issues including depression, anxiety, stress, separation, illness, bereavement, relationship issues, work challenges and caretaking.

About Marie

Trained as a Humanistic and Integrative therapist with Flatstone Institute and a member of the Irish Association of Humanistic and Integrative Psychotherapists (IAHIP). Certificate in REB, Rational Emotive Behavioural Therapy. Certificate in Integrative Body Work.

My Approach

I integrate different approaches depending on the needs of the person, however, being person-centred, authentic, empathic and non-judgemental is what is most important to me while building a therapeutic relationship.

Using neuroscience and attachment as a roadmap for our inner experience, my aim as a therapist is to provide the opportunity for you to explore your inner experience, gaining self-awareness through self-discovery to bring a greater sense of well-being and quality of life—so that you can live a meaningful, fulfilling life, whatever that means for you.

Whether current life challenges or old patterns are resurfacing, having someone to deeply listen and explore together in a safe and caring environment will make a difference in your life.

Areas of Focus:

Abuse, Addiction, Anxiety, Depression, Developmental Trauma, PTSD, Stress, Self Harm, Fear, Anger, Relationship Issues, Internal Confusion.
