Ann Jordan

I’m delighted you’re on this website, because even though you are probably struggling with different parts of your life right now, you’re here because you’re looking for something to change, and you’re looking for a therapist to help you with these changes.

I am a fully accredited Counsellor and Psychotherapist with the Irish Association of Humanistic and Integrative Psychotherapists (IAHIP) and the Addiction Counsellors of Ireland (ACI).

I am an experienced facilitator of Smart Recovery addiction recovery groups and of AFINET’s 5 Step Family Support Programme, for those affected by addiction.

I am a sessional therapist with AHR Employee Assistance Programme, and I am an affiliated counsellor with Dunlewey Addiction Service.

I work with people one-to-one or in groups, both face-to-face and online.

Carolyn Spring (2019) puts it that “people come to therapy, when they’ve experienced a wound, a relational trauma, that has been been perpetrated or caused by another human being. And so people need to have a reparative experience, an opposite experience, where instead of hurt and harm and abuse, people experience care and compassion and empathy. That then has the potential to change the way they view other people, themselves, and the nature of the world”.

The aim of my Psychotherapy and Counselling work is to allow you to deepen awareness of yourself without judgement and with compassionate curiosity.

I centre this therapeutic work around the relational experiences between us in the therapy session, whilst also using your experience of yourself, your relationships and the environment around you. I encourage you to develop an open curiosity for how you are in therapy and in the world around you.

Mary Malone

About Me

I am a fully accredited humanistic integrative psychotherapist trained with Flatstones Institute in 2017. Pet Grief Coach / Counsellor. Certified in inner child healing work, personal development, facilitation skills, integrated specialised tutor trainer, social awareness and community empowerment work.

I am an experienced facilitator with the Social and Health Education Project, where I have led groups in personal development, stress management, assertive communication and community empowerment work. I am also an experienced facilitator with aftercare groups for families coping with addiction, along with providing one-to-one counseling with individuals in addiction treatment centres.

I also have a deep love and respect for animals. I understand the heartbreak of losing a beloved pet. For this reason I also offer a safe space to support clients through the grief process of losing their beloved pet.

Approach

My aim is to provide clients with a safe, compassionate and accepting space that is free from judgment and fully confidential. In my work, I use different psychotherapy approaches depending on the needs of the client. I work at the client’s pace, ensuring they feel seen, heard and supported in addressing the issues that may be arising in their lives. I aim to ensure we are working together towards building the therapeutic relationship. In my work, I focus on providing practical tools and emotional support for those difficult times and helping clients to move forward to a more fulfilling life.

Areas

Stress, anxiety, depression, addiction, families dealing with addiction, abuse, illness, inner child healing, developmental trauma, relationship difficulties (family, personal, work related), grief (both human and pet), along with difficulties that may arise from everyday life.

I work with adults aged 18+, offering face-to-face sessions in my office in Cork, online via Zoom, along with group sessions.

Marie Scully

Trained with Flatstone Institute and a member of the Irish Association of Humanistic and Integrative Psychotherapists (IAHIP). I also have completed training in Rational Emotive Behavioural Therapy (REBT) and Integrative Body Work. I have over 20 years’ experience working with people, from tutor in second chance education to life skills coach with individuals facing homelessness, eventually qualifying as a psychotherapist in 2021.

I integrate different approaches depending on the needs of the person; however, being person-centred, authentic, empathic and non-judgemental is what is most important to me while building a therapeutic relationship.

Using neuroscience and attachment as a roadmap for our inner experience, my aim as a therapist is to provide the opportunity for you to explore your inner experience, gain self-awareness through self-discovery to bring a greater sense of well-being leading to a more fulfilling quality of life.

Whether current life challenges or old patterns re-surfacing again, having someone to deeply listen and explore together in a safe and caring environment will make a difference in your life.

Abuse, Addiction, Anxiety, Depression, Developmental Trauma, PTSD, Stress, Self Harm, Fear, Anger, Relationship Issues, Internal Confusion.
